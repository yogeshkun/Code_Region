What is Insertion Sort ?

   Insertion sort is the sorting mechanism where the sorted array is built having one item at a time. The insertion sort algorithm iterates through an input array and removes one element per iteration, finds the place the element belongs in the array, and then places it there. This process grows a sorted list from left to right. For a contiguous list, the primary costs are the comparisons to determine which part of the sorted portion must be shifted, and the assignments needed to accomplish that shifting of the sorted tail.

   The time and space complexity for bubble sort is:
• Time complexity (Best Case) : O(n)
• Time complexity (Worst Case) : O(n2)
• Time complexity (Average Case) : O(n2)
• Space complexity : O(1)

Algorithm :

Insertion sort Algorithm is as follows:
Step 1: For i = 1 to n-1
Step 2: Set temp = Array[i]
Step 3: Set j = i – 1
Step 4: while temp <=Array[j]
   Set Array[j + 1] = Array[j]
   Set j = j – 1
   [end of step 4]
Step 5: Set Array[j + 1] = temp
   [end of step 1]
Step 6: STOP

Example demonstrating Insertion sort:

Suppose you have the following array, which you have to sort.
13 8 2 22 10 25
In insertion sort, the first two elements are compared.
As there are 6 elements in this array, so you will need 5 steps to sort it.

Step 1:-
Since 8 is smaller than 11, it will change its place among themselves.
8 13 2 1 10 11

Step 2:-
Now, you will compare the next two elements (13 and 2).
Since 2 is smaller than 13, it will change its place among themselves.
8 2 13 1 10 11
After this, 8 and 2 will compare, 2 is smaller than 8, so it will change its place among themselves.
2 8 13 1 10 11

Step 3:-
Now, you will compare 13 and 1.
Since 1 is smaller than 13, it will change its place among themselves.
2 8 1 13 10 11
After this, 8 and 1 will compare, 1 is smaller than 8, so it will change its place among themselves.
2 1 8 13 10 11
After this, 2 and 1 will compare, 1 is smaller than 2, so it will change its place among themselves.
1 2 8 13 10 11

Step 4:-
Now, you will compare 13 and 10.
Since 10 is smaller than 13, it will change its place among themselves.
1 2 8 10 13 11

Step 5:-
Now, you will compare 13 and 11.
Since 11 is smaller than 13, it will change its place among themselves.
1 2 8 10 11 13
Finally the entire array is a sorted array.
